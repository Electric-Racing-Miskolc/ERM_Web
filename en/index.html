    What is Formula Student

About Formula Student

The goal of Formula Student is to design, construct and introduce a formula racing car to a conceptual market and to race that car on several international racing events with the help of all members who have a legal student status. The organization ensures the team members gain valuable insights in their field of expertise and have the opportunity to build channels which enable smooth communication between the students of Formula Student and the supporter companies of the movement. These competitions are held in several countries across Europe, the most notable ones are Germany, England, Austria, and Spain.

The Race

An average Formula Student competition takes up to four days, which consists of two days of scrutineering and assuring that the car complies with various security rules. If it meets all these regulations, the vehicle will be allowed to take apart in the next two days of events. These can be sorted into two main groups: static and dynamic.

Static Events

Static events are presentations which include defending the engineering design, presenting business plan and cost analysis. These give 40% of the total scores.

Dynamic Events

Dynamic events include the “Acceleration”, the so called “Skid Pad” which tests the suspension dynamics of the car on an “8” shaped track, and the “Autocross” which examines the car’s performance on a specific section of the racetrack during measured lap time. Finally, the “Endurance” event where the overall time performance and fuel efficiency of the vehicle are taken under scope on a 22 km long track.

What we do

The team members gather extensive understanding and experience in the automotive industry and various fields of motorsport. They learn the true meaning of teamwork, as well as use their knowledge in practice. The project provides opportunity to become familiar with the usage of the most advanced technologies, as well as offering an indirect way to the industrial companies for the students who are involved.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. How much time does it take to be part of the team?

To join the team, no minimum time commitment is required or expected, but experience has shown that 3-4 hours per week during the training period is essential to acquire the necessary skills. However, the time spent will increase during your time here in direct proportion to the level of tasks you undertake. However, most of the work can be done from home after work, classes or other activities.

2. Is it worth joining the team in the first year?

An average university course lasts 7 semesters. You can only participate in a Formula Student team during this period. As you are a student during this time, we do not expect any professional knowledge, just an open attitude to learning and teamwork. So there is little point in procrastinating and joining in the 3rd or 4th semester, as you can learn everything and have even less time to work in the team.

3. How can I complete my studies while participating in the team? Won't it cause me to slip?

In short, no! Because our team is a small community, you'll also find help with your studies, which will help you more than hinder you. You can do your internships with us and get a thesis/TDK topic. At the same time, you can work on your car and progress with your studies without spending extra time.

4. As an economic student, what tasks can I do and how much technical knowledge am I expected to have?

As a real competitive team we need management. We need to run the team, manage the purchase of materials, manage our finances, manage production and prepare and present various economic presentations for the competition. Technical knowledge is not necessary at all, but you can join anything if you are interested.

5. What knowledge can I gain as part of the team?

An important task of a formula student is to train students to put theoretical knowledge into practice, preferably following the latest trends. In this way it provides competitive knowledge that can be acquired in very few places outside an industrial environment.

6. Can I make money doing this?

Not directly, because it's not a workplace. However, if you put in the necessary effort, you can apply for several scholarships, e.g. Universitas Miskolcensis, ÚNKP, National Scholarship for Higher Education...etc., which can mean 40-50 or 100 thousand HUF per month.

7. Who can join the team?

Everyone with an active university student status.

8. How can I apply for the team and what is the procedure?

If you follow our social media pages or website, we hold at least 1 recruitment every year, which you can apply for online by filling in a google form and submitting a cover letter. You will then be asked to take a test to assess your skills as a technician, and then we will meet you for a face-to-face interview. You will be assessed on the basis of all these criteria. IMPORTANT! The test result carries the least weight and the personal interview the most. If you miss this, but are determined to do what we do, don't be afraid to contact us and there is also an individual procedure to join.

9. What are my obligations if I am part of the team?

This varies depending on the position. There are less and more responsible tasks. The more senior your position, the more people or major parts depend on you, which means you have more responsibilities towards others and the whole team. There is basically no expectation towards you when you join the team. Your first year will be almost entirely about learning and you will always have someone to mentor you in your work.

10. Just interested in driving, looking for a pilot?

We do not need a pilot only. If that's the only thing that motivates you to join, we're not the place for you. However, we used to organise "fun-drive" days where everyone who put the work into the car had the opportunity to try it out.
